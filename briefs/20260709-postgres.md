## Core ideas
Postgres is a powerful relational database management system that offers a range of benefits, including support for complex transactions and constraints. However, it may not be the best choice for every project. When deciding between Postgres and other databases, such as ClickHouse, it's essential to consider the specific needs of the project. For example, if the project requires frequent updates to existing data, Postgres may be a better choice due to its support for transactions and constraints. On the other hand, if the project involves large amounts of immutable data, ClickHouse may be a better option due to its optimized storage and query performance.

To illustrate this, consider the example of Odin's workforce visibility and compliance platform, which uses Postgres to store data related to monitors, incidents, and teams [3]. In this case, Postgres is a good choice due to its ability to handle complex transactions and constraints. However, if the platform were to involve large amounts of immutable data, such as check results, ClickHouse may be a better option.

## Where it fits
Postgres is a key component of the data storage layer in many applications, particularly those that require complex transactions and constraints. It is often used in conjunction with other technologies, such as Kubernetes and GraphQL, to provide a scalable and efficient data storage solution.

## Do this in 5 minutes
Run the following command to create a new Postgres database:
```sql
CREATE DATABASE mydatabase;
```
This command will create a new database named "mydatabase" in your local Postgres instance. You can then use the `\c` command to connect to the database and start exploring its features.
