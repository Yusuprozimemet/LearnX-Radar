## Core ideas
Postgres is a powerful relational database management system that has been widely adopted in various industries due to its reliability, scalability, and flexibility. At its core, Postgres is designed to provide a robust and efficient way to manage and query large datasets. One of the key features of Postgres is its ability to support durable execution, which ensures that database transactions are executed reliably and consistently, even in the face of failures or errors [2]. Additionally, Postgres has been extended to support vector search, which enables developers to build efficient and scalable search systems [5]. For example, ParadeDB's Postgres extension delivers state-of-the-art search capabilities, simplifying the stack and day-to-day operations for its customers [5].

## Where it fits
Postgres fits into the broader stack as a relational database management system, providing a robust and efficient way to manage and query large datasets. Developers would typically reach for Postgres when building applications that require reliable and scalable data storage and retrieval, such as AI-native operations and systems engineering [3][4]. Postgres is particularly useful when working with large datasets, complex queries, and high-performance applications.
